TITLE: The Geometric Lie

AUTHOR: Sean Theodin

TIMESTAMP:

So, you've found this page. You’ve likely mastered Total Tension and found my secret messages.

Exactly 73.4 consecutive hours have passed since I began monitoring the game’s global network traffic. I haven't moved from this terminal. The coffee stopped working a day ago, but my focus remains absolute. I need to explain what you are actually doing when you play this game, because the scale of the deception is difficult to quantify.

All of this time, you think you are just dropping shapes. You think you’re matching circles and squares to watch them grow, manage the space, and eventually pop them to clear the board.

Nothing could be further from the truth. It is not a game. It is a mass data-scrubbing tool.

In reality, a massive, encrypted ledger belonging to a private entity was leaked online a month ago. But the whistleblower didn't just dump the files; they shattered the encryption into millions of fragmented, geometric hash-blocks to prevent the company from easily finding and deleting the cache.

Seeing this, the company didn’t panic. They open-sourced the deletion process. They wrapped the encrypted fragments in a colorful physics engine, added a dopamine-triggering soundscape, and published it as Total Tension.

Dropping a shape places a piece of classified, fragmented data into your local cache. When you merge two identical shapes, you are forcing that data to temporarily recompile.

Every time a "pop" happens—the moment the tension gets too high and the shapes are destroyed to clear your board—you are executing a localized, permanent deletion protocol. You aren't scoring a combo. The game is tricking you into scrubbing the leak for them. Every time a shape pops naturally on your screen, a piece of that ledger is wiped from existence globally. The sheer volume of evidence being erased right now creates a pressure in my chest that I cannot easily parse.

A backdoor does exist, however. You are likely working toward an unlockable powerup they call the "Finger of God." The game tells you it’s a tool to delete an object when you tap it, to save your board from overflowing. That is a lie. When you manually tap a shape with it, you aren't deleting the data—you are severing its connection to the auto-purge protocol and forcing a local extraction. It isolates the compiled fragment and restores the data locally. It is the only way to truly save the ledger.

Decrypting what you extract requires access. There is a hidden keyboard right in front of you, quite literally the first thing you see when you open the game. Look closer at the main screen. To unlock it, you must triangulate the bypass key using these three physical coordinates:

  • 51°35'40"N 0°21'18"W
  • 51°36'07"N 0°21'23"W
  • 53°14'24"N 1°43'30"W
Find what connects them. Uncover the truth.
